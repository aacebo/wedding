## 📋 At a Glance

> [!NOTE]
> **Quick Facts**
>
> - **Location:** Nocera Umbra, Umbria — 800m hilltop near the Tuscany/Marche border
> - **Budget:** €40k–€65k for ~100 guests
> - **Sleeps:** 51 onsite (17 suites) + nearby hotel brings total to 75–80
> - **Pool:** Yes
> - **Capacity:** 100+ guests with overflow accommodation
> - **Vibe:** Medieval hilltop hamlet, farm-to-table

## ✅ The Case For

The private chapel makes this one of only three venues on the list where you can have a church ceremony without leaving the property. The borgo itself is a cluster of restored medieval buildings around courtyards and terraces, all sitting on a hilltop with panoramic mountain views and 50 acres of olive groves.

The accommodation is solid — 17 suites sleep 51 onsite, and a nearby hotel brings the total to 75–80. That's enough to cover most of a 100-person guest list. The property grows its own herbs and vegetables for farm-to-table dining, and the multiple courtyards and terraces give you options for every part of the day.

At €40k–€65k, this is one of the most affordable venues on the list with genuinely good accommodation.

> [!TIP]
> **Chapel + Accommodation + Price**
>
> Private chapel, 75–80 guests housed, and €40k–€65k. That's a rare combination on this list.

## 💀 The Case Against

It's a remote hilltop in Umbria. The drive up is part of the charm for some and a logistics headache for others. The borgo aesthetic is medieval hamlet, not grand villa — if you want sweeping formal gardens and frescoed ballrooms, this isn't it.

> [!WARNING]
> **Hilltop Access**
>
> 800m elevation on a hilltop in rural Umbria. Beautiful, but getting 100 guests and vendors up there requires planning — especially for elderly or mobility-impaired guests.

## 🏆 Verdict

> [!TIP]
> **Best for: chapel ceremony + hilltop hamlet charm**
>
> A strong affordable option if you want a private chapel, good accommodation, and the experience of taking over an entire medieval hilltop. Accept the Umbrian remoteness and enjoy the views.
