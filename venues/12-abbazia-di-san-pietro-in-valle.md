## Location

Ferentillo, Province of Terni, Umbria. In the Nera Valley, between Spoleto and Marmore Falls.

## Why it ranks here

An 8th-century Benedictine abbey — one of the most historically significant venues on this list. Moves up under the new brief because of its extraordinary atmosphere and setting in the lush Nera Valley.

## Estimated all-in budget for ~100 guests

* **€40k–€70k**

## Pros

* 8th-century abbey with extraordinary historical significance
* Two cloisters: Romanesque + 15th-century
* Frescoed church available for ceremonies
* Panoramic gardens and historic refectory
* 18–20 rooms sleeping 43–47 guests
* Wellness area
* In-house wedding planner and shuttle service
* Unique, once-in-a-lifetime setting

## Cons

* No pool onsite (affiliated Casale Santa Croce, 3 km away, has a private pool)
* Remote Umbrian location — far from Florence
* Limited capacity compared to larger estates
* Abbey aesthetic may feel too religious/formal for some

## Verdict

Unmatched historical atmosphere — nowhere else on this list offers an 8th-century abbey with frescoed church ceremonies. The lack of an onsite pool is the main drawback under the new brief.
