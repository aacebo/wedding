A countryside estate in the rolling hills of the Mugello, just north of Florence, Villa di Striano pairs classic Tuscan architecture with olive groves and garden terraces — the perfect backdrop for the day.

A little history

One of the Mugello's oldest villas

With roots reaching back to the Middle Ages, Striano grew over the centuries from fortified country towers into one of the most treasured historic estates in the Mugello.

In 1875 the villa was acquired by the Florentine portrait painter Michele Gordigiani, who had it reimagined in elegant Liberty style by the architect Luigi Del Moro — including the sun-filled Limonaia, shaped by the Renaissance influence of Buontalenti. It later passed to the Berlin baron Robert von Mendelssohn, of the composer's family, who laid out its English-style park with majestic old trees and a natural spring.

Today Villa di Striano is a recognized Historic Residence of the ADSI, the Italian Historic Houses Association.
